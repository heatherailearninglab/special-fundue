AI literacy

Understanding what AI is, where it appears, what it can and cannot do, and how to use it with good judgment.

Generative AI

AI systems that generate new content such as text, images, audio, code, or video based on patterns in training data.

Prompt

The instruction, question, or input you give an AI system to shape its output.

Output

The response the AI system gives back after processing your prompt.

Large language model

An AI model trained on large amounts of text so it can predict and generate language-like responses.

Token

A small chunk of text a language model reads and processes. A token might be a word, part of a word, or punctuation.

Bias

Patterns in outputs that may reflect unfair assumptions, stereotypes, gaps in data, or one-sided perspectives.

Hallucination

When AI produces information that sounds confident but is inaccurate, misleading, or entirely made up.

Training data

The content used to help build and tune an AI model.

Context

The background details that help an AI system understand what you want and respond more usefully.

Accessibility

Designing content so people with different abilities, devices, and support tools can use it successfully.

Responsible AI use

Using AI with care by checking accuracy, considering ethics, protecting privacy, and making thoughtful human decisions.
